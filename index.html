About me

Nice to meet you! I am currently a PhD Student at UC Davis as a NSF Graduate Research Fellow. I'm passionate about providing data-driven solutions to complex but meaningful problems. In particular, my research interests lie broadly at the intersection between data science and water resources engineering. For example, I answer questions such as "How can we improve deep learning models that capture human decision making in water systems?" and "Can we use reinforcement learning to discover new water management strategies that adapt to a changing climate?"

I also have industry experience as an analytical data scientist at Genentech, where I applied my statistical modeling and data analysis skills to support high profile clinical trials for respiratory diseases. I hold a M.S. in Statistics (which supports my interest in data science) and a B.S. in Civil Engineering (where I developed my interest in water resources), both from UC Davis. In my free time, I enjoy making music and finding awesome new restaurants. I love connecting with new people - feel free to reach out!
